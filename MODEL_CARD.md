# Model Card: Wearable Health Telemetry (Synthetic)

This card covers the four modeling tasks in this repository. **All models are trained and
evaluated on synthetic data** and are research/educational artifacts only.

## Intended use
- **Primary:** demonstrate a clean, reproducible ML pipeline (data → features → training →
  held-out evaluation → visualization) for wearable-style cardiovascular signals.
- **Audience:** students, recruiters, and reviewers assessing ML engineering practice.
- **Nature:** exploratory, unpublished coursework/portfolio research.

## Out-of-scope / prohibited uses
- **Any medical or clinical use.** Not for diagnosis, screening, triage, monitoring, or
  treatment decisions. Not a medical device; not FDA-cleared; not clinically validated.
- Inference on **real** wearable/patient data (the models never saw real data and would not be
  valid).
- Any deployment where a wrong output could affect a person's health, insurance, or care.

## Models, inputs, and outputs

| Model | Input features | Output |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| SVM ensemble | `mean_heart_rate`, `std_heart_rate`, `pnn50` | Binary: Normal (0) / Irregular (1) + probability |
| Gradient Boosting | 6 base + 2 derived metrics (HR, HRV, respiratory rate, activity, sleep, stress, `hr_hrv_ratio`, `recovery_score`) | Binary: Low (0) / High (1) risk + probability |
| Neural network (MLP) | 13 HRV summary features (RR-interval statistics + band powers) | 4-class: normal / afib / bradycardia / tachycardia + probabilities |
| Cardio regression | `age`, `resting_hr`, `hrr_1min`, `rmssd` | Continuous: fitness level, VO₂max, cardiovascular age |

All "conditions" and "risk" labels are **synthetic constructs**, not medical findings.

## Training data summary
- **Source:** synthetic, generated by `src/data_processing.py` (NumPy, seed `42`).
- **Sizes:** rhythm 5,000; health-risk 10,000; HRV 4,000; cardio 2,000 (80/20 train/test).
- **Class balance:** rhythm ≈ 60/40 (Normal/Irregular); health-risk ≈ 60/40 (Low/High);
  HRV balanced across 4 classes.
- **Provenance caveat:** classes are drawn from deliberately different distributions and are
  therefore separable by construction.

## Evaluation data summary
- Held-out 20% stratified test split from the same synthetic generators, plus 5-fold CV on the
  training portion. No real or external validation set exists.

## Limitations
- Trained/evaluated **only on synthetic data**; performance does not transfer to real signals.
- **No participant/subject structure**, so no claim about generalization across individuals.
- **Separable-by-design** synthetic classes inflate classification scores; the lower VO₂max R²
  (0.56) shows even the synthetic setting is not uniformly easy.
- Older saved metadata reported slightly different numbers (e.g., SVM 92.4%, VO₂max R² 0.777)
  that did not reproduce; the numbers here come from re-running the code.

## Bias and fairness considerations
- No demographic attributes (sex, race, age distribution beyond a synthetic `age`) were modeled
  or audited. Because the data is synthetic, the models encode **only the generator's
  assumptions**, which may not reflect real population variation. No fairness testing was done,
  and none of these outputs should inform decisions about real people.

## Medical and ethical risks
- **Automation bias / false reassurance:** presenting synthetic-model outputs as health signals
  could mislead a user. The repo mitigates this with explicit disclaimers and synthetic framing.
- **False negatives** (missing an "irregular"/"high-risk" case) are the costliest error type in
  health contexts; the confusion matrices in `assets/` make these visible.
- **Do not** connect these models to real HealthKit data and surface results to users without a
  proper clinical validation and regulatory pathway, none of which exists here.
